Kartma is the embodiment of philanthropy and community. Artist John Bukaty captures dynamic moments at live events on canvas, then donates his original creations.

Kartma was created through a fusion of the words art and karma. The art of live painting has existed since ancient times and follows a tradition of painting a scene as the moment unfolds. Whether it’s a music festival, a sporting event, or charity fundraiser, Kartma aims to create a better world through culture, community, and expression.

John Bukaty has been the drive behind Kartma for more than a decade.

John’s journeys have taken him all over the world - to the far reaches of India painting under a Bodhi tree, to being on stage next to music legend J.J. Cale, to a front row seat at the epic Kansas-Missouri 2012 basketball game where he dazzled the crowd with his exciting, spontaneous approach to painting. Whatever the occasion may be, John brings with him a spirit of inspiration and improvisation that captures the soul of what’s going on around him.

On a momentous trip to India, John’s perspective to art was transformed. The realities of life in a third world country, both the beauty and the hardship, brought immediate insight to his work. That inspiration was the main catalyst for Kartma’s creation and is the heart of its philanthropic motive.

Our core mission is to be a vehicle for meaningful philanthropy - at each event the original artwork is auctioned off for a given charity organization. As such, here are just a few of our charities we've donated to. These numbers will grow with time as more and more money is raised.
